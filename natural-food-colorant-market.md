<p><p>The natural food colorant market comprises several key types, including caramel color, lutein, capsanthin, anthocyanins, and others. Caramel color is derived from heating sugar and is widely used for its browning properties in various foods. Lutein, a carotenoid found in green leafy vegetables, imparts a yellow hue. Capsanthin, sourced from red peppers, provides vibrant red coloring. Anthocyanins, found in fruits like berries, offer shades of purple and blue. Other natural colorants include beet juice and turmeric, catering to diverse culinary applications.</p></p>
