<p><p>The marine energy market comprises several key types: </p><p>1. **Wave Energy** harnesses the kinetic and potential energy from surface waves to generate electricity.  </p><p>2. **Tidal Energy** captures energy from the movement of tides, typically using tidal turbines or barrages.  </p><p>3. **Ocean Thermal Energy Conversion (OTEC)** utilizes the temperature difference between warmer surface water and colder deep water to produce electricity.  </p><p>4. **Other market types** may include salinity gradients and offshore wind energy, complementing the broader marine energy landscape. Each type capitalizes on the ocean's natural dynamics for sustainable energy production.</p></p>
