<p><p>The inoculant market is primarily divided into seed inoculants and soil inoculants. Seed inoculants involve applying beneficial microorganisms directly to seeds, enhancing germination, nutrient uptake, and plant health. These are commonly used for legumes to improve nitrogen fixation. Soil inoculants, on the other hand, are introduced into the soil to promote microbial activity, enhancing soil fertility and plant growth. Both types play critical roles in sustainable agriculture by improving crop yields and soil health.</p></p>
