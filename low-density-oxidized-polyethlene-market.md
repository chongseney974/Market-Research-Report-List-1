<p><p>The Low-Density Oxidized Polyethylene (LDPE) market is categorized primarily into two types: prill and powder. Prill refers to small, spherical granules of oxidized polyethylene, which are typically used for applications requiring uniformity and easier handling. In contrast, the powder form consists of fine particles, ideal for applications demanding high surface area and quick dispersion, such as coatings or adhesives. Both forms hold distinct advantages depending on the application requirements, influencing processing and performance characteristics in various industries.</p></p>
