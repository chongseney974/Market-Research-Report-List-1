<p><p>The Germanium Wafer (Ge Wafer) market is primarily segmented into two types based on conductivity: P-type and N-type. P-type wafers are doped with elements that create "holes" or positive charge carriers, making them effective for applications requiring hole conduction. Conversely, N-type wafers are doped to produce additional electrons, leading to negative charge carriers, which are essential in electronic devices that utilize electron flow. Both types are crucial in semiconductor manufacturing and various optoelectronic applications.</p></p>
